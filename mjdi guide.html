MJDI: Manual Demand Book

Offline Control: Demand Book (Routine & Priority)
1
Purpose of the Demand Book

The Demand Book is your manual fallback and audit trail for demands raised on MJDI. Every demand, routine or priority, should be written in the appropriate book with its serial, demand reference, NSN, quantity, RFD, SPC and account / AinU so that progress can be tracked independently of the system.

DEMAND REGISTER ROUTINE (SPC 13 / 16)
REF: DB-R-023
DEMAND REF 0001234567
DATE 01/06/25 LINE 01
NSN 2520-99-123-4567
QTY / RFD 0004 / 01
SPC 13 (Routine +7 days)
2
Routine vs priority books

Maintain separate books for routine (SPC 13/16) and priority (SPC 05/09) demands. Layout is the same, but priority entries should clearly highlight required-by dates and reasons so that hastening and follow-up can be focussed where risk to output is highest.

3
Closing the loop

Use the Issue Reference and RV/IV columns to show progress: issued, cancelled, FDA, or receipted. Cross-reference RV numbers from MJDI, and annotate reasons where demands are cancelled or replaced. This supports discrepancy, loss and S&R activity by providing a clean paper trail.

When to use Asset Change

Use Asset Change to correct MatCon or resolve small accounting errors, for example changing A1 to R2 after inspection, or recording over-issue / under-issue adjustments. Ensure authority is recorded (for example OC signature or DMC authority) and documentation is filed with the stock record.

MJDI: Discrepancies

Reference: JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 ¬∑ DLF ‚Äì Receipt Discrepancies ¬∑ Local TOP
‚ö†
Principles

All discrepancies between paperwork, MJDI and physical stock must be reported, recorded and actively progressed until resolved. Do not ‚Äúwork around‚Äù discrepancies by forcing receipts or issues to make stock positions look correct ‚Äì follow the discrepancy process and protect the account.

1
Raising a Discrepancy Report (DR)

For receipt discrepancies, raise MOD F445 within the timelines laid down in the DLF. Register the DR in the DR In/Out Register with a serial, and inform the consignor or consignee as applicable. Mandatory investigation DRs must be fully investigated even if the timescale has expired.

MOD F445 Discrepancy Report
DR IN: CA490A
SERIAL DR-IN-023
RAISED Within DLF timescales
Annotate the DR and register with ‚ÄúTIME EXPIRED ‚Äì CONSIGNEE LIABILITY‚Äù where policy requires, and process local loss action if necessary.
2
Investigation and adjustment

Investigate by checking MJDI transactions, physical locations, nearby bins and any related AinU holdings. Use MIS / VITAL as supporting evidence, but not as proof of delivery. When liability is admitted, adjust the account with certified vouchers and update the Loss Register and DR Register with the method of adjustment.

MJDI: Losses & Loss File

Reference: DLF ‚Äì Losses Process ¬∑ SCSI V7.8 ¬∑ Local Loss TOP
1
Identify and register the loss

Once preliminary investigation confirms an item is actually lost, open a loss case and register it in the Loss Register. Determine if the loss is trivial or non-trivial in accordance with the DLF, and calculate the value of loss using the correct valuation rules (for example serviceable, scrap, or special categories).

Record the Demand Book entry, DR reference (if any), and all MJDI transaction references in the loss file index sheet.
2
Investigation, adjustment & reporting

Complete the internal investigation, initiate any external investigation where criminal activity or ACTO items are involved, and then adjust the MJDI account using certified issue vouchers. Update the Loss Register and submit the required periodic loss reports as directed by the DLF and SCSI.

MJDI: Stocktaking & Reconciliation (S&R)

Reference: MJDI Stocktaking ¬∑ JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 ¬∑ Local S&R TOP
1
Prepare for stocktake

Ensure stock locations, bin labels and records are up to date, and that all receipts and issues have been processed on MJDI before generating stocktake lists. Brief counters on procedures, separation of duties and how to record discrepancies.

2
Counting and reconciliation

Compare counted quantities to MJDI holdings, investigate variances and either correct by adjusting stock or raise loss / discrepancy action as required. File all S&R output and counter sheets in accordance with your TOP so that LSA&I and NAO audits can follow the trail.

Ensure AinU naming conventions, supported-supporting relationships and provisioning parameters reflect current reality to avoid spurious U642 outputs and incorrect scales.
