MJDI Guide

Practical, account-focused guidance for RLC Supply Specialists using MJDI on ES Mat accounts.

MJDI: Receipts – U010 / U013 / U014

Menu: Transactions · Receipts · U010 / U013 / U014
1
Understand the source of each receipt

Before receipting on MJDI, identify where the stock is coming from and what evidence you have. This is normally the Issue Voucher (IV) from the consignor, plus any packing lists, delivery notes or, for contracted items, a Certificate of Conformity. The NSN, quantity, condition and document numbers on the paperwork must match what you are about to receipt.

RECEIPTS U010 – Stores Receipt
Account: CA*A
DOC IV 640 / Consignor Ref
EVIDENCE IV, packing list, CoC (if applicable)
Never receipt stock on MJDI without documentary evidence. If paperwork is missing, raise a Discrepancy Report rather than “making the system fit” the delivery.
2
U010 – Normal receipts

Use U010 for routine receipts against an existing demand or open dues. Ensure the NSN, quantity and MatCon match the paperwork and the physical items. Where the demand is earmarked for a particular platform or AinU, confirm that the earmark details are correct before accepting the receipt to stock.

U010 Dues Receipt
Linked Demand: YES
NSN XXX0-99-XXX-XXXX
MATCON A1 (Serviceable)
If the item arrives unfit for use, do not receipt it as A1 and “sort it later” – receipt to the correct unserviceable MatCon or raise a discrepancy with the consignor.
3
U013 / U014 – Local and non-demand receipts

Use U013 and U014 only where authorised by policy, for example local purchase receipts or receipts without a prior demand. These must still be supported by invoices, local purchase forms or MOD direction. Incorrect use creates untraceable stock, weakens the audit trail and will be challenged at LSA&I.

MJDI: Issues – AinU / x-service

Menu: Transactions · Issues · With/Without Issue Request
1
Internal issues to AinU

Articles-in-Use (AinU) accounts represent items loaned to individuals, sections or equipments for a definable period and expected to be returned, not consumed. When issuing to AinU, confirm that the AinU ledger exists, that the borrower is authorised and that the quantity to be issued is reasonable in the context of the task.

ISSUES AinU Issue
Account: CA*A
AinU UNIT Loan – Tools
BORROWER Test unit - fitter
Maintain up-to-date AinU registers and ensure returns are recorded promptly. AinU that is never surrendered will eventually appear as a loss against the account.
2
External issues (x-service)

External issues transfer accountability to another unit or customer. Before processing the issue, confirm the receiving UIN and delivery address using Interrogations → Organisations → Address, and check that the issue has been authorised by the appropriate commander or JAMES task.

MJDI: Manual Demand Book

Governance: Demand Book · CRB · Retention Policy
1
Purpose of the Demand Book

The Demand Book is the unit’s primary manual record of demands raised, progressed and closed. It links need events, MJDI references, COSL entries and any local paperwork. It must be maintained in ink, with no gaps in numbering and corrections made in accordance with CRB assurance guidance, not tippexed or overwritten.

CRB Demand Book
Retention: IAW DLF Rule
ENTRY Serial 001 – NSN – Qty – Demand Ref
The Demand Book must allow an auditor to follow a demand from the original requirement through to receipt, cancellation or disposal using only the references recorded.

MJDI: Clearing Earmarks (EMK)

Menu: Interrogations · Demands · EMK Search
1
Why earmarks matter

Earmarks (EMKs) reserve stock on MJDI against particular demands or platforms. If they are not cleared when issues are made, or if demands are cancelled, they create a false picture of available stock and can lead to unnecessary demands and over-provisioning.

INTERROGATIONS Demands – EMK Search
Filter: EMK Only
Review EMKs regularly. If the associated demand has been satisfied or cancelled, clear the earmark so that stock figures and U642 recommendations remain accurate.

MJDI: Asset Change & MatCon

Menu: Transactions · Asset Change
1
Correct use of Asset Change

Asset Change is used to correct stock condition, value or other accounting attributes where authorised. It is not a shortcut for writing off losses or adjusting stock to match an incorrect count. Every Asset Change must be supported by documentation, an investigation where appropriate, and approval from the Account Holder.

MJDI: External Issues (x-service)

Menu: Transactions · Issues · External Issue
1
Transferring accountability

External issues transfer accountability to another unit, formation or contractor. Confirm the receiving UIN and address, the authority for the issue, and that the quantities and MatCon are correct. Ensure the receiving unit acknowledges the issue so that there is no ambiguity about who now holds the stock.

MJDI: Discrepancy Reports (MOD F445)

Reference: Test unitUNIT TOP Vol 4 Pt 4 · DLF Discrepancy Policy
1
When to raise a Discrepancy Report

A discrepancy indicates a failure somewhere in the Supply Chain that must be investigated and corrected. Raise a Discrepancy Report (DR) when the quantity, condition or document details of a consignment do not match what was expected, and the issue cannot be resolved immediately with the consignor.

DR MOD F445
DR Register
CAUSE Shortage / Excess / Damage / Documentation
Record the facts only. Avoid guessing at causes or blaming individuals – the investigation and consignor feedback will identify root cause and corrective actions.

MJDI: Losses & Loss File

Reference: SCSI Loss Policy · Local Loss TOP
1
Building a loss file

A loss file brings together all documentation relating to a loss: statements, investigations, MJDI vouchers, board of officers reports and the final authority. It must clearly show how the loss was discovered, what action was taken to prevent recurrence and how it was finally written off.

LOSS File Index
Retention: 7 yrs + CY
QTY 0001 VALUE £2,450.00
Record the Demand Book reference, stocktake references and all MJDI transaction references in the loss file index sheet.
2
Writing off a loss

Once authority to write off is granted, raise the appropriate MJDI transaction or certificate voucher and file it in the CRB with the loss case. The write-off must be clearly cross-referenced to the original vouchers and stock records so that there is no break in the audit trail.

MJDI: Stocktaking & Reconciliation (S&R)

Reference: Inv Mgt Stocktaking · JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 · Local S&R TOP
1
Prepare for stocktake

Ensure stock locations, bin labels and records are up to date, and that any outstanding vouchers have been processed on MJDI before generating stocktake lists. Brief counters on procedures, separation of duties and how to record discrepancies.

2
Counting and reconciliation

Compare counted quantities to MJDI holdings, investigate variances and either correct genuine errors or raise losses and discrepancy reports as required. Retain stocktake returns and supporting documentation for the period laid down in the Retention of Materiel Accounting Records rule and your TOP so that LSA&I and NAO audits can follow the trail.

MJDI: UAA & Configuration

Menu: Organisation Maintenance · UAA Role
1
Account configuration

The Unit Application Administrator (UAA) is responsible for maintaining account configuration and user access on MJDI. This includes setting aggregation rules, links to supported units and basic provisioning parameters. These settings drive U642 outputs and other managerial information; they must reflect current task organisation and equipment holdings.

ORG MAINT Scale Calculations
UAA:
MP / LT MP: 30 days · LT: 14 days
DEPENDENCIES All supported units correctly listed
Incorrect configuration cascades into provisioning, so review settings after any change to task org or location.
2
Governance & assurance

Record UAA appointments, configuration changes and key decisions in the CRB. This allows auditors and LSA&I teams to see that MJDI configuration is being actively managed rather than left on default settings.

MJDI: Demands – Need Events & Types

Reference: Inv Mgt Terminology · Inv Mgt SPS · SCSI Vol 7 Ch 3
1
Understanding need events, demands and dues

In Defence inventory policy a need event is any requirement for materiel. A demand is the formal request raised to satisfy that need, and the resulting dues in and dues out are the asset and liability that appear on the account until the demand is satisfied. Dues in represent items ordered but not yet received; dues out represent requirements that exist where there is no stock available to issue. These concepts underpin every MJDI transaction and must match the physical position on the ground.

TERMINOLOGY Demands & Dues
Ref: Inv Mgt Terminology Ch 7
DEMAND Result of a need event raised for materiel
DUES IN Asset – demand placed but not yet received
DUES OUT Liability – requirement waiting to be satisfied
When you receipt or cancel a demand you are changing dues in / dues out balances – always make sure MJDI and the Demand Book tell the same story.
2
Standard Priority System (SPS) and SPCs

The Standard Priority System (SPS) is a joint process that links the urgency of a requirement to a Standard Priority Code (SPC) and a realistic timescale to deliver. As an ES Mat account holder you are expected to select SPCs that reflect the operational requirement and avoid inflating demands “to get it quicker”.

SPS Service Levels
Ref: Inv Mgt SPS Ch 6
SPC 1 / 2 Operational – immediate impact on tasking
SPC 5 / 9 Routine – no immediate impact on readiness
SPC 13 / 16 Unit stocks and replenishment demands
For a routine manual demand using SPC 13, the Required Delivery Date (RDD) is normally set seven days after the demand date. For unit collect demands the RDD is normally two days after the demand date – avoid bringing these forward without justification.
3
Manual demands and stock replenishment

When raising a manual demand on MJDI, ensure you have a completed demand form or JAMES task, that you have screened local stock and cross-servicing options, and that the NSN and codification data are correct. For replenishment of unit stocks, use the Stock Replenishment option which applies the correct SPC and Reason for Demand (RFD) automatically in line with provisioning rules.

TRANSACTIONS Manual Demand
SSC: Routine
SPC 13 – Routine unit demand
STK REPL ☑ (applies SPC 16 for replen)
RFD 3 – Stock replenishment (U642)
Only tick Stock Replenishment when you are replenishing an authorised scale or entitlement and the recommendation is recorded (for example from U642 or local scale review). Do not use it to “shortcut” normal demands.

MJDI: Dues In / Dues Out & Progression

Menu: Interrogations · Demands / Dues · Management Prints
1
Monitoring demands and dues position

Use Demands interrogations to monitor all open demands, their Standard Supply Codes (SSC) and financial due-in position. Focus on high value and high priority items, and check that each demand has a matching entry in the Demand Book so you can show a complete audit trail from requirement to receipt or cancellation.

INTERROGATIONS Demands – EMK / All
Filter: Open only
NSN 2520-99-123-4567
SSC OE – Earmarked FDA 2025-03-15
DUES IN 0004 (1 x EMK / 3 x Pipeline)
Use EMK filters to pick up earmarked demands awaiting issue and act on them daily – this prevents old EMKs sitting on the system with no physical stock held.
2
Progressing delayed demands

Where Forecast Date of Arrival (FDA) has slipped or is unacceptable, use demand interrogations and contact details from the National Codification Bureau or Delivery Agent to chase progress. Check whether the demand has been referred to the provisioner or is held as dues out at primary or secondary level, and record all action in the Demand Book and any COSL or DRS paperwork.

3
Cancelling and amending demands

If a requirement no longer exists, cancel the demand on MJDI, annotate the Demand Book and, if stock has already arrived, treat the items as over-issue / surplus in line with SCSI and local TOP. Never cancel demands simply to clear the U642 or EMK screens; you must be able to show a clear reason and authority for every cancellation.

MJDI: Provisioning & U642 Daily Review

Menu: Provisioning · U642 Daily Provisioning Review
1
Purpose of the U642

The U642 Daily Provisioning Review highlights items where actual demand and holdings differ from the authorised scale. It recommends increasing, decreasing or stopping provision so that the account holder can keep stock levels aligned with real usage while avoiding excessive holdings.

PROVISIONING U642 Review
Frequency: Daily
ACTION Accept / Reject recommendations
FOLLOW ON Creates replenishment demand with RFD 3
U642 must be retrieved and actioned daily. Once a recommendation is accepted, MJDI raises the replenishment demand using Stock Replenishment rules – you must then monitor the resulting dues and receipts like any other demand.
2
Assurance and documentation

File U642 outputs and any local decision logs in the Central Records Branch (CRB) so that LSA&I teams can see how you have managed stock levels. Where you deviate from recommendations, record the reason (for example upcoming deployment or known equipment drawdown) and review the position at the next U642 run.

MJDI: Interrogations – Item Record & Storage

Menu: Interrogations · Item Record · Storage / Ledger
1
Item Record – Storage view

Use Item Record → Storage to see where stock is held, the quantity on hand, condition, and any reservations or earmarks. This is the primary check before agreeing to issues, disposals or DRS activity and must match the physical layout of your storehouses and bins.

ITEM RECORD Storage
Account: CA*A
QOH 0012 (A1)
SAFI 0008 DUES IN 0004
LOCN BAY-01-BIN-03
Always confirm location and MatCon against physical stock and bin labels before receipting or issuing – discrepancies at this stage will propagate into losses later.

MJDI: Interrogations – Demands & Progressions

Menu: Interrogations · Demands
1
Using demand interrogations

Demand interrogations allow you to search by NSN, demand reference, AinU or status (for example EMK) to see the full history of a requirement. Use this to brief commanders on progress, to identify items suitable for COSL reporting and to confirm when demands can be closed and removed from the Demand Book.

MJDI: Interrogations – Organisations & Addresses

Menu: Interrogations · Organisations · Address
1
Finding UINs and contractor details

Use Organisations → Address interrogations to confirm Unit Identification Numbers (UINs), postal addresses and postcodes for supported units and contractors. This is essential when raising external issues, DRS demands or completing COSL returns so that stock moves and documentation can be traced to the right organisation.

MJDI: Management Prints & Fallback

Menu: Reports · Fallback · Management Print
1
Using management prints and fallback sets

Management prints provide a hard-copy snapshot of key MJDI information such as stock holdings, demands, dues positions and AinU. Fallback sets are used when MJDI is unavailable, for example during an outage or exercise, to allow issues and receipts to continue. As an account holder you must know which prints to hold, how often to refresh them and where they are stored.

REPORTS Management Print – Fallback
Frequency: IAW Local TOP
PRINTS Stockholding, Demands, AinU, Locations
STORAGE Sealed fallback pack in CRB / duty desk
When operating on fallback, record all activity in ink on the printed forms. Once MJDI is restored, every transaction must be keyed onto the system and cross-referenced to the fallback record.
2
Retention and assurance

Fallback management prints and any associated worksheets are part of the audit trail. File used sets in the Central Records Branch (CRB) in accordance with the Retention of Materiel Accounting Records guidance and your local TOP, so that LSA&I teams can match MJDI transactions back to the period of fallback working.

MJDI: Amendments – Item Record

Menu: Amendments · Item Record
1
When to amend the item record

Item Record amendments are used to keep codification, description and accounting attributes in line with current policy. Only amend fields that are authorised at unit level; technical data, scaling and codification changes normally originate from the cataloguing authorities and should not be altered locally unless directed.

2
Documenting amendments

Record significant amendments, especially those affecting stock management or valuation, within the CRB and where required on local TOP amendment logs. This demonstrates to auditors that changes are controlled and traceable to a direction, signal or policy reference.

MJDI: Storage Locations & Layout

Menu: Amendments · Storage Locations
1
Aligning system locations to the warehouse

Storage locations on MJDI must mirror the physical layout of your storehouses, bays and bins. When locations change, update MJDI promptly and re-label bins so that stocktaking, issues and receipts can be carried out without confusion. Poorly maintained locations are a common cause of stocktake discrepancies.

MJDI: DRS Overview & Policy

Reference: LSS Class 1 DRS · Local DRS / Carwoods TOP
1
Purpose of the DRS process

The Defence Repair System (DRS) controls how repairable assemblies are removed from equipment, sent to the repair agent and returned to the supply system. On an ES Mat account this usually involves contractor repair organisations such as Carwoods and is closely linked to COSL reporting and JAMES tasks for the platform.

MJDI: Configuring DRS Items on MJDI

Menu: Amendments · Item Record · DRS / Repair Parameters
1
Ensuring DRS data is correct

Where directed by current instruction, check that items subject to DRS have the correct repair codes, repair agent details and return addresses recorded. This allows MJDI documentation to flow correctly between unit, repair agent and depot, and avoids delays when equipment is off-road awaiting repairable spares.

MJDI: Processing a DRS Repair

Reference: LSS Class 1 DRS Process · Local UNIT TOP
1
Linking MJDI to the repair chain

When a repairable item is removed from equipment, ensure there is a JAMES task or local work instruction, raise the appropriate MJDI transaction or DRS documentation, and track the item through to the repair agent and back to stock. File all related paperwork in the DRS / COSL section of the CRB to prove that repairable stocks are being managed correctly.

MJDI: On-Unit Disposals (GXD)

Menu: Transactions · Disposals / GXD
1
When to use GXD

Gateway Disposal (GXD) processes are used when materiel is declared surplus, beyond economical repair or otherwise no longer required for Service use, in accordance with current disposal instructions. Before raising a disposal, confirm that there is no further Service use, that any technical instructions have been followed and that environmental and security considerations are met.

MJDI: Disposal Records, CRB & Retention

Reference: CRB Assurance · Retention of Materiel Accounting Records
1
Maintaining the disposal trail

For every disposal, retain MJDI vouchers, disposal authority, technical instructions and any waste transfer or scrap documentation in the CRB. File these in line with the Retention of Materiel Accounting Records rule so that auditors can verify that stock was removed from the account with proper authority and handled correctly.
