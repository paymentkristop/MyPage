System Overview

VITAL is the pivotal link between Flight ES imports and external logistics organisations (Kuehne-Nagel, Safran, DE&S). It uses unique identifiers to track consignments within the Joint Supply Chain (JSC) at both fixed base and deployed locations.

MJDI: Receipts Guide

Menu: Transactions → Receipts (e.g. U010 / U013 / U014)
1
Pre-receipt checks

Before touching MJDI, confirm the physical receipt aligns with the paperwork. Check: correct UIN, account, NSN, quantity, MatCon, and any packaging damage. Ensure the item actually belongs to your ES Mat account and that any MOD F445 / DR is annotated before proceeding.

MJDI – RECEIPTS ACC: A* | UIN: CA*A
USER: KIRBYK · SESSION: 01
MENU Transactions > Receipts > U010 – Receipt Goods
DOC REF U010 / MOD F 640 / Supplier RV No
CHK UIN / Account / Pack Qty / MatCon match paperwork
NOTE Raise MOD F445 immediately if quantities or NSN differ.
Hint: Do not receipt discrepant stores “just to clear the dues in” – follow DR policy and DLF discrepancy process instead.
📸
Click or drag receipt screen image / U010 here
2
Entering receipt details

On U010 / U013 input the NSC/NIIN, quantity received, MatCon, and RV number. Confirm the dues-in line you are receipting matches the expected DII / requisition. For ES Mat, ensure the correct AINU is selected when required, and that serialised items are recorded in accordance with SCSI V7.8.

U010 – RECEIPT INPUT Store: ES MAT
SPC: 13 · RFD: 3
NSC/NIIN 2520-99-123-4567 QTY 0004 MATCON A1
RV NO 00012345 DUE IN 00012345 / OE
AinU WKSP A* ES MAT LOC BAY-01-BIN-03
Use F8 – Validate before accepting the receipt to catch account / MatCon errors.
3
Post-receipt actions

Once receipted, file the RV and MJDI output in accordance with JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 and your local TOPs. Update any relevant registers (e.g. Demand Book, Planned Repair Register, LVP Register) and physically bin the stock to the correct location, signing and dating bin cards or local location records.

RECEIPT – COMPLETE Stock Record Updated
IV: AUTO · USER: KIRBYK
STOCK QOH: 0012 (Prev 0008)
DOCS FILED RV + MJDI Output → Receipts File (CA*A)
Cross-reference the RV number in the Demand Book against the demand serial to show the demand has been satisfied.

MJDI: Issues Guide

Menu: Transactions → Issues (Internal / External / Loan)
1
Internal issues to AinU

Use internal issues to move stock from the CA*A account to a consuming AinU. Check entitlement, SPC, RFD, MatCon and that the AINU is authorised for that NSN. This supports audit trails and SCSI V7.8 requirements for Articles in Use.

MJDI – ISSUE Internal AinU
SPC: 16 · RFD: 1
NSC/NIIN 2520-99-123-4567 QTY 0002
AinU A* WKSP – FITTER SECT LOC ISSUED
RFD 01 – In-scale operational use
Check the AinU folio matches the CES / entitlement; incorrect AinU selection causes stocktaking discrepancies later.
2
Cross-service / external issues

For x-service issues, confirm the gaining unit’s UIN, SPC and RFD. Use the external issue route as per your External Issues TOP, ensuring the issue voucher prints and is filed, and that remarks clearly show what, to whom, and why (for example “X-service to 16 AA Bde HQ – Ball Joint Kit”).

MJDI – EXT ISSUE Cross-Service
SPC: 13 · NO CHARGE
CONSIGNEE 16 AA BDE HQ (UIN: CA060A)
QTY 0001 MATCON A1
REASON A – No Charge Support
Ensure “Print Issue Voucher at User’s Printer” is ticked so the IV can be signed and filed in the Issues Register.

MJDI: Manual Demand Book

Offline Control: Demand Book (Routine & Priority)
1
Purpose of the Demand Book

The Demand Book is your manual fallback and audit trail for demands raised on MJDI. Every demand, routine or priority, should be written in the appropriate book with its serial, demand reference, NSN, quantity, RFD, SPC and account / AinU so that progress can be tracked independently of the system.

DEMAND REGISTER ROUTINE (SPC 13 / 16)
REF: DB-R-023
DEMAND REF 0001234567
DATE 01/06/25 LINE 01
NSN 2520-99-123-4567
QTY / RFD 0004 / 01
SPC 13 (Routine +7 days)
2
Routine vs priority books

Maintain separate books for routine (SPC 13/16) and priority (SPC 05/09) demands. Layout is the same, but priority entries should clearly highlight required-by dates and reasons so that hastening and follow-up can be focussed where risk to output is highest.

3
Closing the loop

Use the Issue Reference and RV/IV columns to show progress: issued, cancelled, FDA, or receipted. Cross-reference RV numbers from MJDI, and annotate reasons where demands are cancelled or replaced. This supports discrepancy, loss and S&R activity by providing a clean paper trail.

MJDI: Clearing Earmarks (EMK)

Menu: Interrogations → Demands · Transactions → Jobs → Job Management
1
Identify the earmarked NSN

When stock is earmarked during receipting, it will show as SSC “OE / EMK”. Use Interrogations → Demands to search by NSN, identify the OE demand line, and note the requisition number associated with the earmark.

INTERROGATIONS Demands – EMK Search
SSC: OE
NSN 2520-99-123-4567
SSC OE – Earmarked REQN OE/00012345
Record the requisition number in the Demand Book against the original demand entry to show that stock has been earmarked.
2
Clear the earmark via Job Management

Use Transactions → Jobs → Job Management and input the requisition number, ensuring the requisition source is set correctly (for example JAMES). Bring up the job, confirm it is the correct EMK line, then tick the “Issue Stock” checkbox to release the earmark and generate an issue voucher.

Once IV prints, file it with the demand and update the Demand Book entry to show “EMK cleared – IV 00098765”.

MJDI: Asset Change & MatCon

Menu: Transactions → Asset Management → By Item
1
When to use Asset Change

Use Asset Change to correct MatCon or resolve small accounting errors, for example changing A1 to R2 after inspection, or recording over-issue / under-issue adjustments. Ensure authority is recorded (for example OC signature or DMC authority) and documentation is filed with the stock record.

ASSET MANAGEMENT By Item
ACTION: MatCon Change
NSN 2520-99-123-4567 QTY 0001
FROM A1 TO R2
Remarks must clearly state why the change is being made (for example “Downgraded after technical inspection – WO2 Bloggs”).
2
Issue management options

Within Asset Change, use over-issue, under-issue, and issue refusal to correctly adjust stock where demand or issue processing has gone wrong. Ensure the Demand Book and any loss / discrepancy paperwork are updated so that all adjustments can be followed by an auditor.

MJDI: External Issues (x-Service)

Menu: Transactions → Issues → External Issue
1
Pre-issue checks

Confirm the stock is available (Item Record → Storage), that you have authority to support the requesting unit, and that the issue is on the correct account. Validate NSN, MatCon, and quantity against the request, and note the gaining UIN and any tasking reference.

2
Completing the external issue

On the external issue screen, enter the NSC/NIIN, quantity, MatCon and consignee UIN. Select the “Without Issue Request” pathway where appropriate, tick “Issue” and choose the SPC and reason code (for example A – no charge). Add clear remarks that identify the recipient and purpose, and ensure the IV prints and is signed.

EXT ISSUE WITHOUT ISSUE REQUEST
IV: 00098765
CONSIGNEE 16 AA BDE HQ
SPC 13 REASON A – No Charge
File the IV in the External Issues section of the Issues Register, and update any LVP / PR paperwork if the item was part of a planned repair or LVP.

MJDI: Discrepancies

Reference: JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 · DLF – Receipt Discrepancies · Local TOP
⚠
Principles

All discrepancies between paperwork, MJDI and physical stock must be reported, recorded and actively progressed until resolved. Do not “work around” discrepancies by forcing receipts or issues to make stock positions look correct – follow the discrepancy process and protect the account.

1
Raising a Discrepancy Report (DR)

For receipt discrepancies, raise MOD F445 within the timelines laid down in the DLF. Register the DR in the DR In/Out Register with a serial, and inform the consignor or consignee as applicable. Mandatory investigation DRs must be fully investigated even if the timescale has expired.

MOD F445 Discrepancy Report
DR IN: CA490A
SERIAL DR-IN-023
RAISED Within DLF timescales
Annotate the DR and register with “TIME EXPIRED – CONSIGNEE LIABILITY” where policy requires, and process local loss action if necessary.
2
Investigation and adjustment

Investigate by checking MJDI transactions, physical locations, nearby bins and any related AinU holdings. Use MIS / VITAL as supporting evidence, but not as proof of delivery. When liability is admitted, adjust the account with certified vouchers and update the Loss Register and DR Register with the method of adjustment.

MJDI: Losses & Loss File

Reference: DLF – Losses Process · SCSI V7.8 · Local Loss TOP
1
Identify and register the loss

Once preliminary investigation confirms an item is actually lost, open a loss case and register it in the Loss Register. Determine if the loss is trivial or non-trivial in accordance with the DLF, and calculate the value of loss using the correct valuation rules (for example serviceable, scrap, or special categories).

LOSS CASE Registration
Ref: L-2025-001
NSN 2520-99-123-4567
QTY 0001 VALUE £2,450.00
Record the Demand Book entry, DR reference (if any), and all MJDI transaction references in the loss file index sheet.
2
Investigation, adjustment & reporting

Complete the internal investigation, initiate any external investigation where criminal activity or ACTO items are involved, and then adjust the MJDI account using certified issue vouchers. Update the Loss Register and submit the required periodic loss reports as directed by the DLF and SCSI.

MJDI: Stocktaking & Reconciliation (S&R)

Reference: MJDI Stocktaking · JSP 886 Vol 4 Pt 4 · Local S&R TOP
1
Prepare for stocktake

Ensure stock locations, bin labels and records are up to date, and that all receipts and issues have been processed on MJDI before generating stocktake lists. Brief counters on procedures, separation of duties and how to record discrepancies.

2
Counting and reconciliation

Compare counted quantities to MJDI holdings, investigate variances and either correct by adjusting stock or raise loss / discrepancy action as required. File all S&R output and counter sheets in accordance with your TOP so that LSA&I and NAO audits can follow the trail.

MJDI: UAA & Configuration

Menu: Organisation Maintenance · UAA Role
1
Account configuration

The Unit Application Administrator (UAA) is responsible for maintaining account structure, maintenance periods, lead times and search patterns in line with SCSI V7.8. Ensure AinU naming conventions, supported-supporting relationships and provisioning parameters reflect current reality to avoid spurious U642 outputs and incorrect scales.

ORG MAINT Scale Calculations
UAA: KIRBYK
MP / LT MP: 30 days · LT: 14 days
DEPENDENCIES All supported units correctly listed
Incorrect configuration cascades into provisioning, so review settings after any change to task org or location.
2
Governance & assurance

Record UAA appointments, configuration changes and key decisions in a local log, and ensure managerial checks include periodic review of MP/LT, AinU configuration and search patterns. This supports LSA&I evidence and demonstrates that MJDI is being actively managed rather than left on default settings.
